Who is Eric Peterson?

last updated on one of the last days of the 90's
No, I'm not Eric Peterson the Mormon computer scientist in Maryland, Eric Peterson the architect in Boston, Eric Peterson the author of the Cliff's Notes to Pride and Prejudice, Eric Peterson the Amerapress DMV salesman (?) or Eric Peterson the Canadian hovercraft enthusiast. Nope. I'm Eric Peterson the tech writer/musician/Wumpuslover in New York City.

I was born in Washington D.C. in 1964 and grew up in McLean, Virginia (a suburb of Washington, DC) with my parents and older sister. That's me and Sam to the left, Mom and Dad to the right. Mom and Dad now are happily retired in Beaufort, South Carolina and, in fact, Dad designed the library logo on his t-shirt there. Sam lives in Austin and works for the Lesbian/Gay Rights Lobby of Texas. (Yep, we're both gay and we're the only two children. How queer! But Mom and Dad are totally cool with it.)

I went to college in Williamsburg, where I got a B.A. in Music. I went to graduate school in Baltimore, where I majored in horn performance and managed to get an M.M. despite totally giving up on the idea of going pro as a hornist.

I spent the rest of my twenties in Philadelphia, where I worked as a computer geek, did a lot of community theater, played horn, sang semi-professional opera chorus, conducted Philly's gay band and otherwise Learned about Life.

In 1991, I adopted a huge black fluffy thing named Leopold.

In 1994, literally on my 30th birthday, I moved to Manhattan and instantly my life became much more stimulating. Although many people warned me about the perils of New York City, it turned out to be everything I hoped it would be.

In early 1995, after ten years of unsuccessful relationships, I met the most wonderful man in the world. Luckily for us, he had been in the process of co-op hunting anyway when we met, so by the time we were ready to move in together, he had a new home big enough for the both of us. So now I live in historic Jackson Heights, Queens with my Wumpus! We are approaching five years of coupled bliss. A proposal was made and accepted years ago, but the wedding is still in the works.

Professionally I am a technical writer for what was a small software company, which got acquired by a much bigger company.

In 1998, after performing on and off with the Lesbian and Gay Big Apple Corps band, the sister band to the one I conducted in Philadelphia, I became their artistic director. Now the BAC takes up all my time outside of work, but it's worth it. The group is capable of great things, and 1999 saw our performance of Pictures at an Exhibition, as well as my band arrangement of Prokofiev's Piano Concerto #1.

Interests

Scattered thru the Toast Point page you will find many examples of what I'm interested in, but basically they are:

Music

Particularly performance - French horn is my major instrument, with voice and conducting not far behind. I also play all brass instruments (except for trombone - can't get that slide thing to save my life), piano, bassoon, string bass, recorder and (at one point, but not now) clarinet and guitar - none of these necessarily very well. I have a special fondness for chamber music and twentieth-century stuff. I listen to classical, folk, Broadway, 70's rock and some pop.

Theater

Haven't done much lately, but I love to act, and have also done off-stage stuff such as musical direction, stage management, sound and so on. Favorite roles include Bartolo in The Marriage of Figaro, Bob in Beyond Therapy and Carl-Magnus in A Little Night Music. Lately I've been more and more active in the local Gilbert and Sullivan scene, singing Pish-Tush and Giuseppe in concert and participating in sing-along parties. Wumpus and I go to Broadway shows fairly frequently.

Literature

I read a lot, particularly trashy stuff like murder mysteries. Inhaled much science fiction during my formative years. I also write occasionally.
