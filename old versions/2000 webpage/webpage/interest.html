Friends and Family:

  • My father edits the newsletter for The Secular Humanists of the Lowcountry. He also designed the web page initially, but has passed it on to someone who is more into it.
  • My old college roommate and HTML guru maintains the unusually rococo Cul-de-Sac Briceland. Very much worth visiting.
  • Pay a visit to Esther's La-La Land, the virtual reality of my bouncy young friend who was such a delightful host to us in San Francisco.
  • Steven Capsuto, media historian extraordinaire has a great page with all sorts of literary and library-ish links.
  • Miss Alan Lutton is another friend of mine from various directions in Philadelphia, most notably two-stepping.
  • Steven, Alan and I were all sucked into the maw (twice!) of an all-male reading of Claire Booth's The Women. This project came from the mind of the infinitely twisted Chris Connelly. As I told him once, as he was quoting huge chunks of lyrics from Coco, "The rest of us are just trying to be faggots. You're already there."
  • My buddies Gaylord Holder and Marianne Lipson (a spousal team) are bright and interesting persons, have cleverly given up Philadelphia for San Francisco, and on June 30, 1997, produced a little person named Campbell Hannegan Holder! (Note: we got to visit them over Memorial Day.)
  • Frequent Bad Fiction Contest contributor Becky Mushko writes the Peevish Advice column for Blue Ridge Traditions on-line magazine. Check it out!

Gods and Goddesses

Singer Gods - Considering I'm a classically-trained singer and this man sings like a vegetable grater in pain, it's amazing that I love Michael McDonald. And yet, strangely, I do. Probably because he's such a hunka hunka burnin' love (and his music's cool, too!)

Singer Goddesses - My Goddess of Love, the fabulous Christine Lavin will bring great happiness to your life. And so will the up-and-coming folkie who I actually know!, Susan Werner.

Actress Goddesses - Gosh, how the mighty have fallen. The Susan Sarandon Shrine and the Emma Thompson Worship Page seem to have gone the way of all things. When I first created this section, Winona Ryder had 9 pages - now, none of them are left and I don't feel like looking for more. The Jodie Foster Gallery is still up and active, and so is the I love Christina Ricci Too! page...

Still no one has put up a Carol Burnett page. Are there no obsessive queens who care any more?

Gossip

One of the most edifying collection of minds on the planet can be found discoursing in the newsgroup alt.showbiz.gossip. But for goshsake, read the Anti-FAQ and lurk a while before you post, lest you (a) get flamed as YABWFTNOTN, (b) spell out the word l______, or (c) invoke the ASG curse.
