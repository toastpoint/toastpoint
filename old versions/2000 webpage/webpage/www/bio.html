Who is Eric Peterson?

last updated 7/11/98
No, I'm not Eric Peterson the Mormon computer scientist in Maryland, Eric Peterson the Christian singer/songwriter, Eric Peterson the architect in Boston or Eric Peterson the Canadian hovercraft enthusiast. I am certainly not Eric Royce Peterson, author of the incomprehensible Royce's Page of Coolness. Nope. I'm Eric Peterson the tech writer/musician/big ol' fag in New York City.

I was born in Washington D.C. in 1964 and grew up in McLean, VA with my parents and older sister (me and Sam to the left, Mom and Dad to the right). Mom and Dad now are happily retired in Beaufort, SC and, in fact, Dad designed the library logo on his t-shirt there. Sam lived in Beaufort, too, with her fabulous spouse, but they both just moved to Texas, where the spouse's family resides.

I went to college in Williamsburg, , graduate school in Baltimore and moved to Philadelphia for seven years.

In 1991, I adopted a huge black fluffy thing named Leopold.

In 1994, I moved to Manhattan and instantly my life became much more stimulating. Plus, I found a partner, so now I live in Queens with my Wumpus! We are now officially engaged. (In what? Don't ask.)

Professionally I am a technical writer for a small software company, which just moved offices and got acquired by a much bigger company. So we're enjoying newfound pleasures like, um, direct deposit and voice mail.

Interests

Scattered thru the Toast Point page you will find many examples of what I'm interested in, but basically they are:

Music

Particularly performance - French horn is my major instrument, with voice and conducting not far behind. I also play all brass instruments (except for trombone - can't get that slide thing to save my life), piano, bassoon, string bass, recorder and (at one point, but not now) clarinet and guitar - none of these necessarily very well. I have a special fondness for chamber music and twentieth-century stuff. I listen to classical, folk, Broadway, 70's rock and some pop.

Currently, most of my spare time is taken up with my appointment as the Interim Artistic Director (read Temporary Conductor) of the Rainbow Symphonic Winds of New York, a gay community band now entering its 20th season. Stay tuned for the news of whether the RSW has decided to keep me permanently...

I am also the hornist for The Metropolitan Winds, a woodwind quintet. We used to exist solely for an annual week of concerts at the Mt. Washington Hotel in New Hampshire. But this year we have been much more active and will be presenting a concert series at the Bloomingdale School of Music in Manhattan. The Met Winds will soon have its own promotional page up - check here for details.

Theater

Haven't done much lately, but I love to act, and have also done off-stage stuff such as musical direction, stage management, sound and so on. Favorite roles include Bartolo in The Marriage of Figaro, Bob in Beyond Therapy and Carl-Magnus in A Little Night Music. I also did my first Pish-Tush in a concert Mikado just last December. Wumpus and I go to Broadway shows fairly frequently.

Literature

I read a lot, particularly trashy stuff like murder mysteries. Inhaled much science fiction during my formative years. I also write occasionally.
Plus computers (duh), movies, coffee ice cream, etc., etc.
