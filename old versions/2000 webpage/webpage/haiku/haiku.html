Welcome!

Welcome to the Toast Point Haiku Contest! . No fancy prizes, just publication on the Web and gold stars by the ones the sage thinks are particularly wonderful.

What's a Haiku?

It's a poem. According to the HAIKU for PEOPLE! page, the rules are simple:
  1. Each haiku has three lines.
  2. The first line has five syllables.
  3. The second line has seven syllables.
  4. The third line has five syllables again.

They can be written on any topic in any language. You're also supposed to include a "seasonal word", a word or phrase that implies a season, like "falling leaves".The contest splits up into categories if categories become apparent.

What are the House Rules for Haikus?

It has been made clear to Toast Point many times that the five/seven/five rule is not appropriate for English-language haikus, but entries should not stray too far from the path. Any of the following, in no particular order, may tickle the Sage enough to give you a gold star:
  • Attaining the five/seven/five syllable ideal
  • Seasonal word
  • Keeping each line self-contained (no sentences spill to the next line)
  • Striking imagery
  • Humor or clever wordplay
None of these are rules per se, but rather suggestions. Also see how contest entries are edited .
