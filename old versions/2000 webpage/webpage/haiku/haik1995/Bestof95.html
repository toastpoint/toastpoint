Toast Point writes: Contestants started asking me even before the year ended whether there would be a "Best of '95" page set up. I told 'em yes, and the Sage made the selections in the usual timely manner - but I didn't have time to set up the page until spring. Anyway, here are the Sage's choices for Best Haikus of 1995. Most of them are gold star winners already, but some were not - a board review determined their worth after the first judging. Enjoy, and congratulate the winners!

Nature

Joy Hattie writes 10/28

The birds are leaving
Etched against an opal sky
Last flight of autumn

William Tranmer writes 10/11

The sky opens up.
It frees a torrid affair
That started days ago.

John Tower Escargot writes 11/28

Fuchsa-red tinctured

On green, crabby limbs.

However I dream
Of the softly blinding snow
I still know the sun

metamorphosis,

La Mer

Hot night, salty breeze
