Friends and Family:

  • My parents are heavily involved in The Secular Humanists of the Lowcountry.
  • My sister works for the Lesbian and Gay Rights Lobby of Texas in Austin.
  • My old college roommate and HTML guru maintains the unusually rococo Cul-de-Sac Briceland. Very much worth visiting.
  • I met the lovely and talented Susan Poliniak when we were in the chorus of Carmen together years ago in Philadelphia. Now we're both in New York, involved in Gilbert and Sullivan stuff, and she has her fingers in many pies, as you will see.
  • Pay a visit to Esther's La-La Land, the virtual reality of my bouncy young friend who was such a delightful host to us in San Francisco.
  • Steven Capsuto, media historian extraordinaire has a great page with all sorts of literary and library-ish links.
  • Miss Alan Lutton is another friend of mine from various directions in Philadelphia, most notably two-stepping.
  • Steven, Alan and I were all sucked into the maw (twice!) of an all-male reading of Claire Booth's The Women. This project came from the mind of the infinitely twisted Chris Connelly. As I told him once, as he was quoting huge chunks of lyrics from Coco, "The rest of us are just trying to be faggots. You're already there."
  • They haven't updated their sites in ages, but my buddies Gaylord Holder and Marianne Lipson (a spousal team) are bright and interesting persons,and have produced a little person named Campbell Hannegan Holder. We got to visit them in San Francisco over Memorial Day, 1997, but now they're back in Philadelphia, where I met them.)
  • Frequent Bad Fiction Contest contributor Becky Mushko writes the Peevish Advice column for Blue Ridge Traditions on-line magazine. Check it out!
