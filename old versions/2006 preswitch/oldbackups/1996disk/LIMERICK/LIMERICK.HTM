The casual browser is hereby notified that any sections marked "naughty" are indeed potentially offensive and should be avoided by the pure of heart. The serious composer of limericks knows full well that the form lends itself to naughtiness and will jump wholeheartedly into the spirit of the thing. Newcomers may want to read the Instructions to see how the contest works. All poets may also want to check out the Hints on Limerick Structure and the How Dare You Edit My Limerick! sections.

Policy Note

The Sage judges the contest entries. Toast Point edits them. Entries are not censored; however, Toast Point and the Sage may decide to put them behind a "warning link" if they are horribly offensive.

Since contest entries frequently comment on previous entries, be aware that you may get flamed via limerick. If this might upset you, Toast Point suggests that you use a pseudonym when submitting. And you are welcome to flame back, as long as it's in limerick form!
