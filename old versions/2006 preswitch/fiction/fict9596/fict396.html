Cassandra A. Goduti begins a Young Adult mystery novel 3/30

Had I but known the ancient, dank history of Castle Windvere, I would never have entered gaping maw of its stained glass front doors. My friends, Josclyn and Alex - missing. I don't know where I lost them, they just disappeared while we were walking. The doors creaked to the castle, sounding like fingernails scratching a chalkboard. Cobwebs hung at every corner and in front of almost every doorway. If I hadn't been so foolish, I would have never have gotten us into this mess. If it hadn't been for that stupid bet. Now I had to find my friends to leave. It never occurred to me until now, that leaving may not be an option. My palms began to sweat, Could I possible leave without my friends? This castle holds tales that I said I could uncover because I didn't believe this house to be haunted or possessed, just lived in by someone none of us knew. The number of people I had seen coming and going from this castle out of strange doors and corners, just didn't appear right to me. I just have to tread carefully.

ERCS begins a Science Fiction novel 3/28

The house had lain dormant for decades, perched there on Coogan's Bluff. Nothing had disturbed its slumber since the burning time. That is, until now. The Brianites were a rare breed of Extra-Terrestrials from the planet Bob. In addition to their repulsive appearance, the Brianites lacked even the slightest amount of real intelligence and quite often lost their way in the universe when attempting to explore their own planet. The small group of Brianites flew from galaxy to galaxy, searching for their home of Bob. Alas, the Brianites gave up their search for planet Bob and settled for a place of similar consistency; Earth. "What is this?" Captain Brian1 asked his fellow Brianites, lowering the ship in front of the deserted house. "It appears to be a silent, harmless mass, Captain." Brian2, the wisest, carefully observed. The Brianites stared in awe at this sight as they gathered around the windows. "Then we must attack it!" Captain Brian1 triumphantly exclaimed.

Steve begins a Science Fiction novel 3/28

With one swift dive, Joel cut the water like a knife through butter, or a spoon through jello. At first, moving through what he thought was water was easy. As he continued he felt a slight resistance through every stroke. He noticed that the water was turning from a clear blue to a mint green color before his eyes. All of a sudden a dark green shadow was beneath him in the water. He immediately started to flail around in the solidifying mint green substance.

Daniel begins a Horror novel 3/20

"Oh, fudge," Mary cried as her roadster sputtered to the curbside. "My radiator is leaking," the attractive slender young woman realized, "and I'll never get to the hop in time to meet Stan!". "I can't mess things up, Stan is the best man I've ever met", she thought. In a sudden shock of self-pity, Mary got out of the car, sat down on the rough gravel, and cried. As cars rushed by, her tears turned to puddles.

Suddenly, an old Ford van stopped to give her aid. A charming young man rolled down the window and asked "Do you need a ride somewhere, babe?" Mary's world suddenly got bright again. She jumped in the passenger's seat and thanked the nice young man. All she could think about was Stan, and how she longed to be with him. A big smile appeared, as well as a strong feeling of euphoria. "I can't thank you enough for giving me a ride!", Mary shouted. The young man replied in a cocky voice "This ride ain't free, lady". And stopped the van.

Before Mary could react, a hand from behind her appeared and pulled her to the rear of the vehicle. She began to scream while being stripped of her blouse. The driver and another young man began to pull off her tight jeans. Wearing only a bra and panties, Mary began squirm. The driver began to punch her in the stomach while his friend kicked her crotch repeatedly. After beating her to a slight squirm, they peeled off her panties, it was a group effort, and they liked what they saw.

Mythletoe begins a Mystery 3/20

With one swift dive, Joel cut the water like a knife through butter, or a spoon through jello. The crowd gasped when a the water became tie-dyed with crimson. Joel's body floated to the top like a dead fish. A woman screamed at the sight and the onlookers hushed.

Joel's sister set aside her grief for the moment and took charge of the situation as her cop instincts had told her to do. She dashed down to poolside and dove in to retrieve the body. She dragged the limp corpse to the dry cement and examined the wound. She found that it was a bullet hole. "Who would have shot Joel?", she asked herself although she knew the answer. She looked up at the crowd for some clue and spotted a man running toward the gates. She knew there was no way for her to catch him, so she stayed with the body. Security guards came rushing at her and she withdrew her soggy badge from her back pocket and informed them that she was Detective Joline Barnsworth, the victim's sister. She told them that she saw what she thought was the assailant running out and a screech of tires confirmed her suspicions. "Why had Joel gotten himself mixed up with those thugs?" she asked herself.

Dean Cooze begins a Pulp Novel 3/3

Flicking her cigarette contemptuously and tossing her thick mane of hair, Thyla adjusted the straps of her halter top and barked with her trademark raucous, sneering laughter - laughter known to have unmanned many a suitor. "Fred, get the hell out here!", she snarled, grinding the butt beneath her spiked heel.

"Whuh" came the sound from the bathroom. Rolling her eyes, Thyla whumped on the door and said "Hurry up, you asshole, we'll be late! And we don't want Moldy in our face." A muffled expletive was heard as the toilet flushed and Fred, bleary-eyed stumbled out in a cloud of foul odor. "Geez, you stinking pig! Whadya eat last night, burritos?" Thyla sneered as she stomped out of the room.
