Whyte9ght writes 07/19/97

Jiak kopi and go pak tor
Talk like that in Singapore
England, Hokkien and Malay
Spiak like that at work and play
This is called our Singlish, lor

The poet comments, "Definitions:

  • Jiak kopi : lit. "Eat coffee". Means to have a friendly drink and chat with a friend. Originated from Hokkien. Eg. Want to go jiak kopi?
  • go pak tor: Courting. Eg. My parents pak tor for 7 years before getting married
  • England: Corruption of "English" Eg. You think your England very the good is it?
  • Hokkien: A Chinese dialect from the present day Fujian province in China
  • Malay: A language from South East Asia
  • Spiak: Corruption of "speak"
  • Singlish: The local variety of English. Contraction of "Singapore English"
  • lor: Added to end of sentences to give emphasis
These definitions are my own, and subject to debate. The grammartical forms are true to life and are corrupted, if any one minds. "
